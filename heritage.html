The Name

Why Silvertown?

Silvertown, located in the Royal Docks area of East London, has a history that is essentially a timeline of the British Industrial Revolution ‚Äî from swampy marshland to a global manufacturing powerhouse, through devastating tragedy, and finally into a massive modern regeneration project.

The area is named after Samuel Winkworth Silver, a clothing manufacturer who moved his waterproof factory to this isolated marshland in 1852. His factory became the catalyst for an entire industrial town ‚Äî and eventually, a global hub of connection.

The year Samuel Silver founded the district that would become the most important industrial hub of the Victorian era.
